## What is Attribution?

Attribution is the process of identifying which marketing touchpoints contribute to conversions. When a customer:

1. Clicks a Facebook ad
2. Visits your website
3. Leaves and comes back via Google
4. Signs up for a trial
5. Eventually makes a purchase

**Who gets credit?** Attribution answers this question.

Without proper attribution:
- **Facebook** reports: "We drove this conversion!"
- **Google** reports: "No, we did!"
- **You**: Confused about what actually works

## Key Concepts

### Touchpoint

Any interaction a customer has with your marketing:
- Ad click
- Email open
- Social media engagement
- Website visit
- Form submission

### Conversion

The desired outcome you're measuring:
- Lead (form submission)
- Signup
- Purchase
- Subscription

### Attribution Window

How far back to look for touchpoints (default: 30 days).

A customer who clicked an ad 45 days ago won't have that touchpoint attributed if your window is 30 days.

### Channel

The source of traffic:
- **Paid Social** - Facebook, Instagram, TikTok ads
- **Paid Search** - Google, Bing ads
- **Organic Social** - Non-paid social media
- **Organic Search** - SEO traffic
- **Email** - Email campaigns
- **Direct** - Direct traffic (no referrer)
- **Referral** - Links from other websites
