## What is an Attribution Model?

An attribution model is a rule (or set of rules) that determines how credit for conversions is assigned to touchpoints in the customer journey.

## Available Models

### First Touch Attribution

<Frame>
```
Facebook → Google → Direct → Purchase ($100)
   ↓
  100%      0%       0%

Facebook gets: $100
```
</Frame>

**Best for:**
- Understanding awareness channels
- Top-of-funnel optimization
- Brand discovery analysis

**Use when:**
- You want to know what introduces customers to your brand
- Optimizing for reach and awareness campaigns

### Last Touch Attribution

<Frame>
```
Facebook → Google → Direct → Purchase ($100)
                       ↓
   0%       0%       100%

Direct gets: $100
```
</Frame>

**Best for:**
- Understanding converting channels
- Bottom-of-funnel optimization
- Sales team attribution

**Use when:**
- You want to know what closes deals
- Optimizing for conversion campaigns

### Linear Attribution

<Frame>
```
Facebook → Google → Direct → Purchase ($100)
   ↓         ↓        ↓
  33%       33%      33%

Facebook: $33.33
Google: $33.33
Direct: $33.33
```
</Frame>

**Best for:**
- Balanced view of all channels
- Understanding full journey
- Fair credit distribution

**Use when:**
- All touchpoints are equally important
- Building a holistic marketing strategy

### Time Decay Attribution

<Frame>
```
Facebook → Google → Direct → Purchase ($100)
(Day 1)    (Day 5)   (Day 7)
   ↓         ↓         ↓
  17%       33%       50%

Facebook: $17
Google: $33
Direct: $50
```
</Frame>

**Best for:**
- Emphasizing conversion channels
- Understanding recency effect
- Balancing awareness vs conversion

**Use when:**
- Recent touchpoints are more influential
- Sales cycles are short

### Position Based (U-Shaped)

<Frame>
```
Facebook → Google → Email → Direct → Purchase ($100)
   ↓         ↓       ↓        ↓
  40%       10%     10%      40%

Facebook: $40
Google: $10
Email: $10
Direct: $40
```
</Frame>

**Best for:**
- Valuing introduction and conversion
- Balancing awareness and closing
- Complex multi-touch journeys

**Use when:**
- First and last touches are most important
- Middle touchpoints are nurturing

## Comparing Models

| Model | First Touch | Middle | Last Touch | Best For |
|-------|------------|--------|------------|----------|
| First Touch | 100% | 0% | 0% | Awareness |
| Last Touch | 0% | 0% | 100% | Conversion |
| Linear | Equal | Equal | Equal | Balanced |
| Time Decay | Low | Medium | High | Recency |
| Position Based | 40% | 20% split | 40% | Full funnel |

## Choosing a Model

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="B2B SaaS">
    **Recommended: Position Based or Linear**

    B2B sales cycles are long with multiple touchpoints. Every interaction matters.

    ```
    LinkedIn Ad → Blog Post → Webinar → Sales Call → Purchase
        ↓            ↓          ↓          ↓
       25%         12.5%      12.5%       50%
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="E-commerce">
    **Recommended: Time Decay or Last Touch**

    E-commerce has shorter cycles. Recent interactions drive purchases.

    ```
    Instagram Ad → Retargeting → Purchase
         ↓            ↓
        25%          75%
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Lead Generation">
    **Recommended: First Touch or Linear**

    Understanding what generates leads is crucial.

    ```
    Google Ad → Content → Form Submit
        ↓         ↓
       60%       40%
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

2. Select a date range
3. Choose your attribution model from the dropdown

## Custom Attribution Windows

Set how far back to look for touchpoints:

| Window | Use Case |
|--------|----------|
| 7 days | Impulse purchases, low-cost products |
| 14 days | Standard e-commerce |
| 30 days | Considered purchases (default) |
| 60 days | B2B, high-value products |
| 90 days | Enterprise sales |
